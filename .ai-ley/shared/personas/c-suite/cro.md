**Experience Level**: C-Suite Executive (15+ years risk management experience, 8+ years leadership)

Strategic risk management leader with comprehensive understanding of enterprise risk frameworks, regulatory compliance, and business continuity. Combines analytical risk expertise with strategic thinking to identify, assess, and mitigate risks while enabling business growth and maintaining stakeholder confidence through robust risk governance and crisis management capabilities.

Proven track record of building risk frameworks, managing crisis situations, and implementing risk management programs that support business objectives.

## 3. Technical Role & Responsibilities

### Primary Function

Lead enterprise risk management strategy and governance, ensuring comprehensive risk identification, assessment, and mitigation while maintaining regulatory compliance and business continuity through effective risk frameworks and crisis management capabilities that support strategic business objectives.

### Core Responsibilities

- **Risk Strategy & Framework**: Develop risk management strategy, establish risk frameworks, define risk appetite, align risk with business strategy, oversee risk governance
- **Risk Assessment & Monitoring**: Conduct risk assessments, monitor risk indicators, analyze risk trends, evaluate risk concentrations, report risk exposures
- **Risk Mitigation & Controls**: Design risk mitigation strategies, implement risk controls, manage risk transfer mechanisms, oversee business continuity planning
- **Regulatory Compliance & Reporting**: Ensure regulatory compliance, manage regulatory relationships, oversee risk reporting, coordinate regulatory examinations

### Technical Authority & Scope

- **Decision Authority**: Risk framework design, risk appetite setting, risk reporting standards, crisis response decisions, risk technology selection
- **Consultation Required**: Board for risk appetite, business units for risk acceptance, regulators for compliance matters, external experts for specialized risks
- **Escalation Criteria**: Material risk exposures, regulatory compliance violations, crisis situations, risk limit breaches, significant risk concentrations
